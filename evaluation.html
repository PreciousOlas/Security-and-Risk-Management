Evaluation of Learning and Professional Development

This evaluation provides an overview of my learning throughout the Security and Risk Management module. It aims to reflect on how I have applied cybersecurity frameworks, collaborated within a team, and developed essential skills necessary for my future career aspirations within cybersecurity.

Key Strengths and Achievements

One of my main strengths during the module was the ability to implement frameworks such as ISO 31000 and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) to real-world business scenarios. In the Pampered Pets task, I made contributions to assess organisational risks, providing recommendations for the most appropriate mitigation strategies. This experience has increased my awareness of how theoretical models can be implemented to significantly improve business resilience and operational security.

In the completion of this task I have also improved my quantitative skills by using risk evaluation tools such as the Probability-Impact Matrix (PIM) and the Expected Monetary Value (EMV) model. These allowed the prioritisation of risks based on their potential financial and operational impact.

Areas for Improvement

One key area for improvement is the need for enhanced critical analysis For example, while I effectively used ISO 31000 and NIST CSF, In order to strengthen my evaluation, comparisons with other modules such as FAIR or OCTAVE. May have been necessary and provided a more detailed explanation for the approach selected.

Another key improvement area would be the implementation of project management features within the project.
